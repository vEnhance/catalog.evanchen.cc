## Unit Overview

AIME Geo is focused on Computational Geometry containing some of the hardest
geometry problems that have appeared on AIME along with a mix of other computational contests such as ARML,
HMMT, PUMaC, NIMO, and some mock AIMEs.
There are a sprinkle of olympiad problems featuring many of the same strategies
needed to solve the computational problems.

The B version of the unit is an average difficulty B unit (harder than [Sympoly](/A/sympoly)/[EV](/C/evcomp),
easier than [Equality](/C/equality)/[Elem Geo](/G/elemgeo)) while the D unit is on the easier side of D units.

## Notable Problems

* 2014 HMNT Team 10: Pretty involved problem using angle chasing and a lot of similar triangles.
* [2017 CMIMC G9](https://aops.com/community/p7592269) and [AIME II
  2020/15](https://aops.com/community/p15680911):
  Problems with a similar idea of finding nice properties about the diagram at
  hand and involving a lot of angle chasing. Both are possible to do with trigonometry as well.
* [AIME 2016/15](https://aops.com/community/p5966212): Just pure synthetic geometry.

## Advice

If you are preparing for AIME and want to hone your late AIME geo skills, this is an amazing unit to do.
