## Philosophy

This unit creates the idea that there are two types of computational number theory problems:

* "Problems which are actually algebra or combinatorics problems that happen to
  use integers in some superficial way."

* Just plain number theory.

For this reason, the problem set contains a wide variety of problems.
Most noticeable are the three (and a half) sections that pertain to computational problems,
olympiad problems, diophantine equations, and a short one on **ACTUAL AIME PROBLEMS**!
However, the two types of number theory problems are prevalent in all parts
(except arguably diophantine equations.)
