This unit covers a variety of high powered theorems and concepts found in both computational and olympiad settings. 

Included are tricks to finding integer or rational families of solutions on strange curves using the theory of [Pell equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pell%27s_equation), [Vieta Jumping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vieta_jumping), and a brief touch at elliptic curves in the walkthrough. 

Also in this unit are results relating to the behavior and distribution of primes: [Quadratic Reciprocity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quadratic_reciprocity), [Zsigmondy theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zsigmondy%27s_theorem), [Bertrand postulate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bertrand%27s_postulate), and [Dirichlet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dirichlet%27s_theorem_on_arithmetic_progressions) are mentioned. 

Heavy NT can be quite tough. It could be considered an extension of [Expon NT](https://otis.evanchen.cc/wiki/units/list-of-number-theory-units/expon-nt/) in terms of theory, and is also similar to [Prime Exponents](https://otis.evanchen.cc/wiki/units/list-of-number-theory-units/prime-exponents/) and [Orders](https://otis.evanchen.cc/wiki/units/list-of-number-theory-units/orders/) as careful analysis mod p is key to many problems.
