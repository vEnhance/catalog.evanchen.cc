## Content and Brief Overview

The theoretical portion of this unit consists of mainly $2$ big results.
The famous result of Turan's Theorem and one of it's corollaries: Mantel's lemma.
Having said that, it is advised that the reader doesn't get to worked up with
the 2 above ideas and on the contrary still looks out for equality cases and
exploit all opportunities to carry out induction.

### Pre-requisites

 Readers should be comfortable with ideas of induction and analyzing equality cases.
 Introduction to Graph Theoretical terminologies is not in the scope of this unit,
 so the reader should get acquainted with the same if they're not fluent with them already.

### Prominent Problems

- (From Mathematical Reflections)
A Tetrahedron free graph $G$ has $3n$ vertices.
What is the maximum possible number of triangles in $G$?
- ISL 2004 C8
- Korea 2015 #6
- Canada 2020 #5
- (China TST 1987) Let $G$ be the graph with
  $2n$ vertices and $n^2 + 1$ edges where $n \ge 2$.
  Prove that $G$ contains two triangles with a common edge.
